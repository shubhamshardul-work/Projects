## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[PEFT-Arena: Understanding Parameter-Efficient Finetuning from a Stability-Plasticity Perspective](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28819v1)**
This research delves into the fundamental trade-offs of Parameter-Efficient Finetuning (PEFT), crucial for optimizing large language models. Understanding the balance between retaining pre-trained knowledge and adapting to new tasks will directly inform how researchers and practitioners efficiently fine-tune models, impacting resource allocation and performance in real-world AI applications.

### **[VLMs May Not Globally Enhance Human Alignment over LLMs During Natural Reading](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28818v1)**
This paper challenges the assumption that Vision-Language Models (VLMs) inherently improve human alignment in language processing. It suggests that simply adding visual data doesn't automatically make text representations more human-like, prompting researchers to re-evaluate multimodal integration strategies for truly effective human-AI interaction.

### **[Self-Improving Language Models with Bidirectional Evolutionary Search](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28814v1)**
This work explores novel search methods, specifically bidirectional evolutionary search, for creating self-improving language models. Moving beyond traditional approaches, this concept could lead to more robust and autonomously evolving AI systems, pushing the boundaries of what agentic AI can achieve in terms of learning and adaptation.

### **[Calibrating Conservatism for Scalable Oversight](http://arxiv.com/abs/2605.28807v1)**
This research addresses the critical control problem of agentic AI systems, focusing on how humans can maintain meaningful oversight over increasingly autonomous AI. Developing methods to calibrate conservatism is paramount for safety and ethical deployment, offering vital insights for designing future AI governance frameworks and ensuring human-in-the-loop control.

### **[OmniVerifier-M1: Multimodal Meta-Verifier with Explicit Structured Recalibration](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28805v1)**
As multimodal large language models become central to AI, reliable and fine-grained verification of their visual outcomes is essential. OmniVerifier-M1 proposes a new approach to this challenge, which is vital for scaling generalist foundation models reliably and ensuring their outputs are accurate and trustworthy across different modalities.
