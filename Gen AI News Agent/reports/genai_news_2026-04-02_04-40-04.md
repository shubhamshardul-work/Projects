## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Therefore I am. I Think](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01202v1)**
This paper delves into the fundamental question of whether large language reasoning models "think" before deciding or decide before "thinking." The research presents evidence to detect this sequence, offering crucial insights into the cognitive processes and decision-making architectures of advanced AI.

### **[$\texttt{YC-Bench}$: Benchmarking AI Agents for Long-Term Planning and Consistent Execution](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01212v1)**
As LLM agents tackle increasingly complex, multi-step tasks, the YC-Bench benchmark addresses the critical challenge of maintaining strategic coherence over long horizons. This work is vital for evaluating agents' ability to plan under uncertainty, learn from delayed feedback, and execute consistently, pushing towards more robust and autonomous AI.

### **[HippoCamp: Benchmarking Contextual Agents on Personal Computers](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01221v1)**
HippoCamp introduces a novel benchmark specifically designed to assess AI agents' capabilities in multimodal file management on personal computers. This fills a gap in existing benchmarks, focusing on practical, real-world interactions with local environments and advancing the development of truly helpful desktop AI assistants.

### **[Universal YOCO for Efficient Depth Scaling](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01220v1)**
This research explores Universal YOCO as a method for efficient depth scaling, aiming to improve how standard Transformers handle inference-time compute. The work is critical for boosting the reasoning and agentic proficiency of LLMs while making test-time scaling more computationally feasible and sustainable.

### **[CliffSearch: Structured Agentic Co-Evolution over Theory and Code for Scientific Algorithm Discovery](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01210v1)**
CliffSearch proposes a novel approach for scientific algorithm discovery, combining agentic co-evolution over both theoretical hypotheses and practical code implementations. This iterative system promises to accelerate the scientific process by enabling LLM-guided search systems to better stress-test and revise proposals, leading to more robust discoveries.
