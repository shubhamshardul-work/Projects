## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Pretraining Data Can Be Poisoned through Computational Propaganda](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15267v1)**
This research exposes a critical vulnerability: poisoning pretraining data can introduce harmful, difficult-to-detect behaviors into large language models. It underscores the urgent need for robust data integrity checks and mitigation strategies to ensure the trustworthiness of AI systems.

### **[SceneBind: Binding What and Where Across Vision, Audio and Language](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15265v1)**
SceneBind introduces an omni-modal representation that achieves joint semantic and 3D spatial understanding across vision, audio, and language. This work pushes the frontier of multimodal AI, moving towards more holistic and context-aware perception of realistic scenes.

### **[RoboTTT: Context Scaling for Robot Policies](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15275v1)**
RoboTTT addresses a key limitation in robot foundation models by enabling context scaling for visuomotor policies, allowing for longer interaction histories. This breakthrough is crucial for developing more complex, adaptive, and robust robotic behaviors in dynamic environments.

### **[Partition, Prompt, Aggregate: Statistical Self-Consistency in Language Models](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15277v1)**
This paper explores a novel method to enhance the reliability and consistency of language model outputs through statistical self-consistency via partitioning, prompting, and aggregation. It offers a promising avenue for improving the robustness of in-context learning.

### **[SearchOS-V1: Towards Robust Open-Domain Information-Seeking Agent Collaboration](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15257v1)**
SearchOS-V1 tackles the challenge of agent performance degradation in long interaction histories during open-domain information-seeking tasks. This research is vital for developing more robust and collaborative AI agents capable of sustained, complex problem-solving.
