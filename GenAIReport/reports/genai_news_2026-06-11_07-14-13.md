## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Reroute, Don't Remove: Recoverable Visual Token Routing for Vision-Language Models](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12412v1)**
This paper addresses the significant computational burden of visual tokens in Vision-Language Models (VLMs) by proposing a novel routing mechanism rather than simple removal. Researchers should investigate this approach for developing more scalable and performant multimodal models, crucial for handling high-resolution visual inputs efficiently.

### **[Context-Driven Incremental Compression for Multi-Turn Dialogue Generation](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12411v1)**
Tackling the quadratic cost of attention in long conversational histories, this research introduces methods for context-driven incremental compression of dialogue. This is a vital step towards building more efficient and context-aware conversational agents that can maintain long-term coherence without incurring prohibitive computational costs.

### **[Doc-to-Atom: Learning to Compile and Compose Memory Atoms](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12400v1)**
This work aims to overcome the limitations of long input sequences in Large Language Models by breaking down documents into "memory atoms" for more efficient processing. This novel approach could lead to significant advancements in document understanding and multi-step reasoning for complex, lengthy tasks.

### **[Redesign Mixture-of-Experts Routers with Manifold Power Iteration](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12397v1)**
This paper focuses on optimizing the cornerstone component of Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models: the router. Improvements in expert selection through manifold power iteration could lead to more efficient and effective MoE architectures, enhancing the scalability and performance of next-generation large language models.

### **[Which Models Are Our Models Built On? Auditing Invisible Dependencies in Modern LLMs](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12385v1)**
This critical research highlights the growing problem of "invisible dependencies" in LLM training pipelines, where models increasingly rely on other models for data generation, filtering, and evaluation. It underscores the urgent need for better provenance tracking and auditing tools to ensure transparency, fairness, and security in the complex ecosystem of AI development.
