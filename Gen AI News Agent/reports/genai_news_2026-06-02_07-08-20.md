## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Mitigating Perceptual Judgment Bias in Multimodal LLM-as-a-Judge via Perceptual Perturbation and Reward Modeling](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02578v1)**
This research tackles the crucial problem of perceptual judgment bias in multimodal LLMs used as evaluators, particularly when visual evidence conflicts with other inputs. By proposing methods like perceptual perturbation and reward modeling, it aims to enhance the reliability and trustworthiness of MLLM-as-a-judge systems, a vital step for their broader adoption in automated assessment tasks.

### **[Permissive Safety Through Trusted Inference: Verifiable Belief-Space Neural Safety Filters for Assured Interactive Robotics](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02562v1)**
This paper introduces a novel approach to ensuring safety in interactive robotics by developing verifiable belief-space neural safety filters. By enabling robots to make safe and efficient decisions under human-induced uncertainty, this research is fundamental for deploying autonomous systems in complex, human-centric environments, paving the way for more reliable and trustworthy human-robot collaboration.

### **[ProtoAda: Prototype-Guided Adaptive Adapter Expansion and Geometric Consolidation for Multimodal Continual Instruction Tuning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02576v1)**
ProtoAda addresses the challenge of continual learning for Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), proposing a method for adaptive adapter expansion and geometric consolidation. This innovation allows MLLMs to continually acquire new vision-language capabilities without forgetting previous knowledge, significantly enhancing their adaptability and long-term utility in dynamic real-world applications.

### **[AdaCodec: A Predictive Visual Code for Video MLLMs](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02569v1)**
AdaCodec introduces a predictive visual code designed to enhance the efficiency of video Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) by leveraging temporal redundancy in video data. This novel approach promises to significantly reduce computational overhead and improve processing speed for video MLLMs, making them more practical for real-time applications and large-scale video analysis.

### **[IntraShuffler: A Privacy Preserving Framework for Heterogeneous DP Federated Learning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02563v1)**
IntraShuffler presents a privacy-preserving framework for Heterogeneous Differential Privacy (HDP) in Federated Learning, allowing clients to customize privacy budgets based on data sensitivity. This research is critical for enabling secure and compliant collaborative AI training across diverse datasets, addressing a major hurdle for deploying federated learning in privacy-sensitive sectors like healthcare and finance.
