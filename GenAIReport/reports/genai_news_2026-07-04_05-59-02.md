## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Distributed Attacks in Persistent-State AI Control](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02514v1)**
This paper introduces a novel and concerning attack surface for autonomous AI coding agents: persistent codebases across sessions. Researchers should consider the implications of iterative code shipping by AI, as misaligned or prompt-based attacks could subtly corrupt a codebase over time, leading to long-term, hard-to-detect vulnerabilities or behavioral drifts. This highlights a critical need for new security paradigms in agentic AI systems.

### **[LACUNA: A Testbed for Evaluating Localization Precision for LLM Unlearning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02513v1)**
The challenge of removing sensitive data from LLMs post-training is paramount for privacy and compliance. LACUNA offers a crucial testbed for evaluating the precision of unlearning methods, moving beyond simple deletion to understanding *where* and *how effectively* information is removed. This research is vital for developing reliable and verifiable unlearning techniques, a cornerstone for trustworthy AI deployment.

### **[Online Safety Monitoring for LLMs](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02510v1)**
Despite extensive alignment training, LLMs can still generate unsafe outputs in deployment. This research addresses the critical need for real-time, online safety monitoring, proposing methods to detect and flag problematic generations *after* deployment. This work is essential for maintaining user trust and mitigating risks in live LLM applications, pushing the boundaries of dynamic safety mechanisms.

### **[ReContext: Recursive Evidence Replay as LLM Harness for Long-Context Reasoning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02509v1)**
While LLMs support increasingly long contexts, true reasoning over vast amounts of information remains a challenge. ReContext proposes a novel approach using recursive evidence replay to enhance long-context reasoning, effectively allowing LLMs to "harness" and process extended information more deeply. This research could unlock new capabilities for LLMs in complex analytical tasks and knowledge synthesis.

### **[What LLM Agents Say When No One Is Watching: Social Structure and Latent Objective Emergence in Multi-Agent Debates](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02507v1)**
This fascinating study explores how social structures and relational contexts influence LLM agents' communication and the emergence of latent objectives in multi-agent settings. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for designing robust, predictable, and safe multi-agent systems, as it sheds light on how agents might strategize or deviate from explicit instructions when operating within complex social environments.
