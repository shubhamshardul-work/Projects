## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[HANDOFF: Humanoid Agentic Task-Space Whole-Body Control via Distilled Complementary Teachers](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06493v1)**
This research explores advanced whole-body control for humanoid robots, a critical step towards deploying them in complex real-world environments. The concept of "distilled complementary teachers" offers a novel approach to managing the intricate interface between task planning and physical execution, pushing the boundaries of robotic autonomy.

### **[TempoVLA: Learning Speed-Controllable Vision-Language-Action Policies](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06491v1)**
TempoVLA addresses a fundamental challenge in robot manipulation: dynamically adjusting execution speed based on task risk. By enabling Vision-Language-Action models to learn speed-controllable policies, this work promises safer and more efficient robot interactions, particularly in scenarios requiring both rapid transit and precise contact.

### **[Code2LoRA: Hypernetwork-Generated Adapters for Code Language Models under Software Evolution](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06492v1)**
Code2LoRA presents an innovative solution for adapting code language models to evolving software projects by generating repository-level context through hypernetworks. This research is vital for improving the practical utility of LLMs in software development, allowing them to maintain relevance and accuracy as codebases change.

### **[TailLoR: Protecting Principal Components in Parameter-Efficient Continual Learning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06494v1)**
TailLoR introduces a new method for parameter-efficient continual learning, focusing on preserving critical information (principal components) during model updates. This work is significant for developing AI systems that can learn continuously from new data without suffering from catastrophic forgetting, enhancing their long-term adaptability and performance.

### **[Pretraining Recurrent Networks without Recurrence](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06479v1)**
This paper challenges conventional wisdom by proposing a method to pretrain recurrent neural networks without relying on traditional recurrence. Such a breakthrough could revolutionize the training of sequential models, potentially leading to more stable, efficient, and scalable RNN architectures by circumventing the limitations of backpropagation through time.
