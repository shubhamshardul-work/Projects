## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Tokenisation via Convex Relaxations](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22821v1)**
This paper proposes a novel approach to tokenization, a fundamental component of NLP, by framing it as a convex optimization problem. This shift from greedy heuristics to a more globally optimal mathematical framework could lead to more efficient and robust tokenization schemes, potentially improving the performance and interpretability of large language models.

### **[Vector Policy Optimization: Training for Diversity Improves Test-Time Search](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22817v1)**
This research explores how training language models for diverse outputs can significantly enhance their performance in complex inference-scaling search procedures. It suggests a paradigm shift in reinforcement learning for LLMs, where optimizing for a spectrum of valid responses, rather than a single "best" one, leads to more robust and adaptable agents capable of navigating novel environments.

### **[The Matching Principle: A Geometric Theory of Loss Functions for Nuisance-Robust Representation Learning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22800v1)**
This paper introduces a unified geometric theory for designing loss functions that promote robustness against various "nuisance" factors like domain shifts or occlusions. By providing a foundational framework, it could simplify the development of more generalizable and resilient AI models across diverse applications, moving beyond ad-hoc solutions for specific robustness challenges.

### **[MOSS: Self-Evolution through Source-Level Rewriting in Autonomous Agent Systems](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22794v1)**
MOSS presents a significant step towards truly autonomous agent systems that can learn and adapt post-deployment by rewriting their own source code. This self-evolution capability could dramatically reduce the need for human intervention in maintaining and improving AI agents, paving the way for more resilient and continuously improving intelligent systems.

### **[Gated DeltaNet-2: Decoupling Erase and Write in Linear Attention](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22791v1)**
This work refines linear attention mechanisms by decoupling the "erase" and "write" operations within their recurrent state, addressing a key challenge in maintaining fixed-size memory while processing long sequences. This advancement could lead to more efficient and performant linear attention models, making them more competitive with traditional softmax attention for long-context tasks.
