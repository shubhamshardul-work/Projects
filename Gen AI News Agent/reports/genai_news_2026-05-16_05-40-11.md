## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Is Grep All You Need? How Agent Harnesses Reshape Agentic Search](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15184v1)**
This paper explores novel paradigms for agentic search, questioning traditional approaches and proposing how LLM agents can fundamentally reshape information retrieval. Researchers should delve into the implications of "agent harnesses" for building more efficient and autonomous systems that can navigate and reason over vast corpora, potentially leading to breakthroughs in knowledge discovery and task automation.

### **[FutureSim: Replaying World Events to Evaluate Adaptive Agents](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15188v1)**
Evaluating AI agents in dynamic, open-ended environments is a critical challenge, and FutureSim offers a promising solution by replaying world events to measure adaptability. This research provides a valuable framework for rigorously testing how agents learn and adjust to new information, which is essential for developing robust and reliable AI systems deployed in real-world, unpredictable scenarios.

### **[Eradicating Negative Transfer in Multi-Physics Foundation Models via Sparse Mixture-of-Experts Routing](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15179v1)**
Scaling Scientific Machine Learning (SciML) to universal foundation models is often hindered by "negative transfer," where learning one task interferes with another. This paper proposes Sparse Mixture-of-Experts routing as a solution, offering a significant step towards building more versatile and powerful scientific AI models capable of handling diverse physical phenomena without performance degradation.

### **[When Are Two Networks the Same? Tensor Similarity for Mechanistic Interpretability](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15183v1)**
Mechanistic interpretability aims to break down AI models into understandable components, but verifying that two parts perform the same computation remains a challenge. This research introduces tensor similarity measures to address this, providing a crucial tool for researchers seeking to deeply understand, compare, and ultimately trust the internal workings of complex neural networks.

### **[EntityBench: Towards Entity-Consistent Long-Range Multi-Shot Video Generation](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15199v1)**
Multi-shot video generation faces a significant hurdle in maintaining consistent characters, objects, and locations across extended narratives. EntityBench proposes a new benchmark and approach to tackle this "entity consistency" problem, pushing the frontier of generative AI towards creating more coherent and believable long-form visual stories, a key step for advanced content creation.
