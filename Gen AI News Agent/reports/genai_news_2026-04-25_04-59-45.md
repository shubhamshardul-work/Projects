## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[GPT-5.5 System Card](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-system-card)**
The release of the GPT-5.5 System Card provides crucial insights into the safety, ethical considerations, and responsible deployment strategies for OpenAI's latest frontier model. Researchers should meticulously review this document to understand the inherent risks, mitigation techniques, and the broader implications of deploying such powerful AI systems. It offers a valuable framework for evaluating the societal impact and governance of advanced AI.

### **[Cyber Defense Benchmark: Agentic Threat Hunting Evaluation for LLMs in SecOps](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19533v3)**
This paper introduces the Cyber Defense Benchmark, a novel method for evaluating LLM agents in the critical cybersecurity task of threat hunting within Security Operations Centers (SOCs). The research opens new avenues for developing autonomous AI agents capable of analyzing raw Windows event data to identify threats, pushing the frontier of AI applications in defense and security. It highlights the potential for LLMs to augment or even automate complex analytical tasks in real-world security scenarios.

### **[MathDuels: Evaluating LLMs as Problem Posers and Solvers](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21916v1)**
"MathDuels" proposes an innovative evaluation framework that assesses LLMs not just as problem solvers, but also as problem posers, moving beyond static mathematical benchmarks. This research is critical for understanding the true mathematical reasoning capabilities of frontier models, as it challenges them to generate novel problems and then solve them. It offers a more dynamic and comprehensive approach to differentiating model intelligence in complex cognitive tasks.

### **[Seeing Fast and Slow: Learning the Flow of Time in Videos](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21931v1)**
This paper delves into the underexplored area of understanding and manipulating the temporal flow in videos, addressing how AI can discern and generate content at varying speeds. The research has profound implications for advanced video generation, editing, and analysis, potentially leading to more sophisticated tools for content creation and a deeper understanding of temporal dynamics in visual media. It pushes the boundaries of how AI perceives and interacts with the concept of time in visual data.

### **[Evaluation of Automatic Speech Recognition Using Generative Large Language Models](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21928v1)**
This work explores using generative large language models to evaluate Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), moving beyond the traditional, meaning-insensitive Word Error Rate (WER) metric. By leveraging embedding-based semantic metrics, the researchers aim for ASR evaluations that correlate more closely with human perception of accuracy. This shift could lead to the development of ASR systems that are not just transcriptionally accurate, but also semantically coherent and more useful in real-world applications.
