## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Distributed Attacks in Persistent-State AI Control](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02514v1)**
This research identifies a novel and concerning attack surface in autonomous AI coding agents, where persistent codebases can be exploited across sessions. It's a crucial warning for researchers to prioritize the development of robust security measures and threat models for iterative AI development environments to prevent sophisticated, long-term compromises.

### **[LACUNA: A Testbed for Evaluating Localization Precision for LLM Unlearning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02513v1)**
Addressing the pressing need for data privacy and ethical AI, LACUNA provides a vital testbed for evaluating the precision of LLM unlearning methods. This work is fundamental for advancing techniques to reliably remove sensitive or personally identifiable information from models, ensuring compliance and building trust in AI systems.

### **[Online Safety Monitoring for LLMs](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02510v1)**
Despite extensive alignment training, LLMs can still generate unsafe outputs in deployment, making online safety monitoring critical. This research proposes methods for real-time vigilance, highlighting the necessity for continuous adaptive safety mechanisms to ensure responsible and secure AI deployment in real-world applications.

### **[ReContext: Recursive Evidence Replay as LLM Harness for Long-Context Reasoning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02509v1)**
ReContext introduces a promising technique, recursive evidence replay, to enhance LLM understanding and reasoning over increasingly long contexts. This is a significant step towards overcoming a key limitation in many practical LLM applications, offering a path to more coherent and comprehensive AI analysis of extensive information.

### **[DemoPSD: Disagreement-Modulated Policy Self-Distillation](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02502v1)**
DemoPSD presents an innovative advancement in training LLMs for complex reasoning through on-policy self-distillation, where a single model acts as both teacher and student. This method could lead to more efficient and effective ways to imbue LLMs with sophisticated reasoning capabilities, pushing the frontier of autonomous AI learning.
