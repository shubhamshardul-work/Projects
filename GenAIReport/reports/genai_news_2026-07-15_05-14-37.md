## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Do AI Agents Know When a Task Is Simple? Toward Complexity-Aware Reasoning and Execution](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13034v1)**
This research explores a critical limitation of current LLM agents: their inability to assess task complexity, often leading to inefficient over-execution. Developing "complexity-aware" reasoning could revolutionize agent efficiency and robustness, paving the way for more intelligent and resource-optimized autonomous systems.

### **[TerraZero: Procedural Driving Simulation for Zero-Demonstration Self-Play at Scale](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13028v1)**
TerraZero presents a novel approach to training autonomous driving agents through procedural simulation, enabling scalable reinforcement learning without relying on extensive real-world demonstrations. This work is crucial for advancing the safety and versatility of self-driving technology by creating diverse and realistic training environments.

### **[Audio-Native Speech Recognition with a Frozen Discrete-Diffusion Language Model](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13013v1)**
Challenging the dominance of autoregressive decoders, this paper investigates using a discrete diffusion language model for audio-native speech recognition. This innovative method, which refines a whole transcription rather than token-by-token generation, could lead to more efficient and robust automatic speech recognition systems.

### **[The Seriality Gap in Video Diffusion Models](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13031v1)**
Researchers have identified a "seriality gap" in video diffusion models, revealing their limitations in predicting sequential causality in dynamic scenes. Understanding and addressing this gap is fundamental for improving the realism and physical accuracy of future video generation and understanding models.

### **[The Spectrum Is Not Enough: When Context Helps Time-Series Forecasting](http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13006v1)**
This paper challenges conventional wisdom in time-series forecasting, demonstrating that spectral predictability scores alone are insufficient and that adding longer-term context significantly improves forecasting accuracy. This research pushes the boundaries of predictability assessment, urging a more holistic approach to time-series analysis.
