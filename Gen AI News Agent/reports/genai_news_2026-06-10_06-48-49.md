## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[When to Align, When to Predict: A Phase Diagram for Multimodal Learning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11190v1)**
This paper delves into the fundamental question of when cross-modal alignment (CA) versus cross-modal prediction (CP) is more effective for multimodal representation learning. Researchers will find this systematic understanding crucial for designing more robust and efficient multimodal AI systems, offering a "phase diagram" to guide architectural choices based on specific data and task characteristics.

### **[A Unifying Lens on Supervised Fine-Tuning Through Target Distribution Design](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11189v1)**
This research proposes a unifying lens on Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) by focusing on target distribution design, addressing issues like noisy or non-unique tokens in training data. Academics and practitioners alike will find this work valuable for developing more effective SFT strategies, potentially leading to models that are better aligned with desired behaviors and less susceptible to training data imperfections.

### **[Evaluation Cards: An Interpretive Layer for AI Evaluation Reporting](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09809v2)**
"Evaluation Cards" propose a standardized, interpretive layer for reporting AI evaluation results, aiming to bring consistency to the currently fragmented landscape of benchmarks and model cards. This initiative is critical for researchers seeking to reliably compare and understand AI model performance, fostering greater transparency and reproducibility across the field.

### **[EEVEE: Towards Test-time Prompt Learning in the Real World for Self-Improving Agents](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11182v1)**
EEVEE introduces a novel multi-dataset test-time prompt learning framework for LLM agents, enabling self-improvement in real-world, dynamic task streams. This work pushes the frontier of adaptive AI, offering a significant step towards agents that can continuously learn and refine their behavior in deployment, a key challenge for robust, long-lived AI systems.

### **[Predicting Future Behaviors in Reasoning Models Enables Better Steering](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11172v1)**
This paper explores predicting future behaviors in large reasoning models to enable better steering, addressing the challenge of unexpected model outputs while preserving quality. Researchers focused on AI safety and interpretability will find this approach valuable for developing more controllable and predictable advanced AI systems, moving towards more reliable human-AI collaboration.
