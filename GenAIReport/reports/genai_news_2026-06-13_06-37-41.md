## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[EurekAgent: Agent Environment Engineering is All You Need For Autonomous Scientific Discovery](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13662v1)**
EurekAgent proposes a paradigm shift, suggesting that engineering the environment is paramount for LLM-based agents to achieve autonomous scientific discovery. This research moves beyond mere model improvements, focusing on creating optimal interactive spaces for agents to propose, validate, and iterate scientific solutions, opening new avenues for AI in research.

### **[EvoArena: Tracking Memory Evolution for Robust LLM Agents in Dynamic Environments](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13681v1)**
EvoArena addresses the critical challenge of deploying LLM agents in dynamic, real-world environments by focusing on memory evolution. This work is vital for developing agents that can adapt and maintain performance over time, pushing beyond static benchmark evaluations towards more robust and practical real-world AI deployments.

### **[Learning to Reason by Analogy via Retrieval-Augmented Reinforcement Fine-Tuning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13680v1)**
This paper introduces a novel approach to significantly improve LLM reasoning by analogy through retrieval-augmented reinforcement fine-tuning. It suggests that conventional RAG is insufficient for complex reasoning tasks, opening new avenues for grounding models in external knowledge in a more sophisticated, context-aware manner.

### **[Mana: Dexterous Manipulation of Articulated Tools](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13677v1)**
Mana tackles the formidable challenge of dexterous manipulation of articulated tools in robotics, a complex area due to the need for coordinating internal degrees of freedom and managing contact-rich interactions. This research pushes the boundaries of robotic control, with profound implications for advanced manufacturing, surgical robotics, and human-robot collaboration.

### **[SpatialClaw: Rethinking Action Interface for Agentic Spatial Reasoning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13673v1)**
SpatialClaw rethinks the action interface for agentic spatial reasoning, a fundamental challenge for Vision-Language Models (VLMs) in understanding 3D environments. This work is crucial for developing agents that can accurately perceive and interact with physical spaces, paving the way for more capable embodied AI and advanced robotics.
