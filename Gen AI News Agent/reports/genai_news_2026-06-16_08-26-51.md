## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[ArXiv] The Value Axis: Language Models Encode Whether They're on the Right Track**
This research delves into the fascinating concept of language models internally tracking their "value axis," essentially an internal gauge of whether their current strategy is likely to achieve their goals. Understanding and leveraging this intrinsic self-assessment mechanism could be a breakthrough for developing more reliable, self-correcting, and truly agentic AI systems, moving beyond mere pattern matching to genuine strategic reasoning.

### **[ArXiv] Context-Aware RL for Agentic and Multimodal LLMs**
Addressing a critical limitation where LLMs struggle to pinpoint decisive evidence in long or complex contexts, this paper proposes Context-Aware Reinforcement Learning. This approach is vital for enhancing the robustness and accuracy of agentic and multimodal LLMs, enabling them to perform more effectively in tasks requiring deep contextual understanding and precise information extraction, such as analyzing tool traces or subtle data points.

### **[ArXiv] Exact Posterior Score Estimation for Solving Linear Inverse Problems**
This paper presents a significant technical advancement in leveraging diffusion and flow-based models for solving linear inverse problems by accurately estimating the posterior score. This research has profound implications for fields like medical imaging, signal processing, and scientific computing, where recovering original data from noisy or incomplete observations is crucial, promising more precise and efficient solutions.

### **[ArXiv] Geometric Action Model for Robot Policy Learning**
Introducing a Geometric Action Model, this research aims to equip generalist robot policies with enhanced reasoning capabilities about 3D physical interactions between objects, cameras, and robot actions. This is a crucial step towards developing more intelligent and adaptable robots that can better understand and navigate complex real-world environments, moving beyond simple task execution to truly intelligent physical interaction.

### **[ArXiv] Hierarchical Advantage Weighting for Online RL Fine-Tuning of VLAs from Sparse Episode Outcomes**
This work tackles the challenge of sparse rewards in online Reinforcement Learning for Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, where robot fine-tuning often yields only binary success/failure outcomes. By proposing Hierarchical Advantage Weighting, the research offers a method to derive more granular per-transition supervision, significantly improving the efficiency and effectiveness of fine-tuning robot policies in real-world, low-data environments.
