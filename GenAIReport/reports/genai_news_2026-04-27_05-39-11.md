### **[Agentic World Modeling: Foundations, Capabilities, Laws, and Beyond](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22748v1)**
This paper delves into the foundational challenge of "agentic world modeling," which is crucial for AI systems to effectively interact with and manipulate complex environments. Researchers will find this a vital read for advancing the capabilities of autonomous agents, exploring how they can build and utilize internal models of their surroundings to achieve sustained goals. It lays groundwork for more general and robust AI systems.

### **[Spend Less, Fit Better: Budget-Efficient Scaling Law Fitting via Active Experiment Selection](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22753v1)**
Addressing the multi-million-dollar cost of fitting scaling laws for large AI models, this research proposes an active experiment selection method to achieve budget-efficient optimization. This work is highly relevant for researchers and institutions seeking to optimize their resource allocation for large-scale model training and development, potentially accelerating progress by making expensive empirical research more accessible and efficient. It offers a practical approach to a significant economic bottleneck.

### **[How Do AI Agents Spend Your Money? Analyzing and Predicting Token Consumption in Agentic Coding Tasks](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22750v1)**
This paper provides crucial insights into the economic implications of deploying AI agents, specifically by analyzing and predicting token consumption in coding tasks. Researchers focused on agent efficiency and cost optimization will find this invaluable for designing more resource-aware agents and developing strategies to manage the rapidly growing token expenditures associated with complex agentic workflows. It bridges the gap between agent design and operational costs.

### **[Beyond N-gram: Data-Aware X-GRAM Extraction for Efficient Embedding Parameter Scaling](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21724v2)**
This research explores novel methods for improving embedding parameter efficiency by moving "Beyond N-gram" with data-aware X-GRAM extraction. For researchers working on fundamental model architectures and scaling, this paper offers a promising path to overcome limitations in token-indexed lookup tables, potentially leading to more memory-efficient and performant large language models without sacrificing practical gains. It addresses a core challenge in scaling model capacity.
