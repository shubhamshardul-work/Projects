### **[ArXiv] Gaze Heads: How VLMs Look at What They Describe** (http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14703v1)
This research delves into the interpretability of Vision-Language Models, revealing specific attention mechanisms that allow them to "see" what they describe. Understanding these internal workings is crucial for building more transparent and reliable multimodal AI systems.

### **[ArXiv] ClinHallu: A Benchmark for Diagnosing Stage-Wise Hallucinations in Medical MLLM Reasoning** (http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14697v1)
Addressing a critical challenge, this new benchmark aims to improve the trustworthiness of medical multimodal LLMs by systematically diagnosing hallucinations. This is a vital step towards deploying AI safely and effectively in clinical decision support.

### **[ArXiv] Persona-Pruner: Sculpting Lightweight Models for Role-Playing** (http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14695v1)
Researchers are exploring methods to create more efficient, specialized language models for specific applications like role-playing chatbots. This work is significant for developing highly performant and resource-optimized AI agents capable of consistent, stylized interactions.

### **[ArXiv] AdaSR: Adaptive Streaming Reasoning with Hierarchical Relative Policy Optimization** (http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14694v1)
Moving beyond static reasoning, this paper introduces a paradigm for adaptive streaming reasoning, allowing models to process and react to dynamic, real-world inputs. This approach is essential for AI systems operating in continuously evolving environments.

### **[ArXiv] Learning Coordinated Preference for Multi-Objective Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning** (http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14693v1)
This research tackles the complex problem of cooperative decision-making in multi-agent systems with conflicting objectives. It offers new insights into how AI teams can learn to coordinate preferences, crucial for advanced collaborative AI applications.

### **[ArXiv] CORA: Analyzing and bridging thinking-answer gap in Multimodal RLVR via Consistency-Oriented Reasoning Alignment** (http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14691v1)
This work focuses on enhancing the reasoning capabilities of multimodal reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards by aligning internal thought processes with final answers. Improving consistency is key to developing more robust and trustworthy multimodal AI.
