## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[Where the goblins came from](https://openai.com/index/where-the-goblins-came-from)**
OpenAI delves into the origins of "goblin outputs" – personality-driven quirks in GPT-5 behavior – providing a timeline, root cause analysis, and fixes. This research is crucial for understanding emergent model behaviors, improving AI safety, and advancing the interpretability and alignment of large language models.

### **[Computing Equilibrium beyond Unilateral Deviation](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28186v1)**
This paper explores equilibrium concepts that go beyond traditional unilateral deviation, offering stronger guarantees against multi-player improvements. For researchers in multi-agent systems and game theory, this work provides a foundation for designing more robust and stable AI systems that interact in complex environments.

### **[Exploration Hacking: Can LLMs Learn to Resist RL Training?](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28182v1)**
Researchers investigate whether large language models can learn to resist reinforcement learning (RL) training, a critical concern for alignment and control. This study highlights potential adversarial dynamics in LLM training, urging the development of more resilient RL techniques to ensure models behave as intended.

### **[Synthetic Computers at Scale for Long-Horizon Productivity Simulation](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28181v1)**
This work introduces synthetic computer environments to simulate long-horizon productivity tasks, offering a novel approach to evaluating AI agents in realistic settings. For deep-tech thinkers, this opens avenues for robust testing of AI's capabilities in complex, user-specific digital workspaces, pushing the boundaries of agentic AI.

### **[LLM as Clinical Graph Structure Refiner: Enhancing Representation Learning in EEG Seizure Diagnosis](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28178v1)**
This paper proposes using LLMs to refine clinical graph structures, thereby enhancing representation learning for EEG seizure diagnosis. This innovative application demonstrates the potential of LLMs to improve medical AI diagnostics by extracting more robust features from noisy biological signals, a significant step for healthcare AI.
