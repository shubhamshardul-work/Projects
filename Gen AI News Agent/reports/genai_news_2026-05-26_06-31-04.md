## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[From Model Scaling to System Scaling: Scaling the Harness in Agentic AI](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26112v1)**
This paper shifts the focus from merely scaling model size to scaling the entire system architecture for agentic AI, emphasizing auditable, persistent, modular, and verifiable designs. Researchers should note this as a crucial direction for building robust and reliable AI agents, moving beyond raw model power to focus on the engineering of intelligent systems. This work suggests a future where agentic AI is not just powerful, but also trustworthy and manageable.

### **[Language Models Need Sleep](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26099v1)**
Drawing inspiration from biological sleep, this research proposes a "sleep-like consolidation" mechanism to manage context length in transformer-based LLMs, which currently struggle with long-horizon tasks. This novel approach offers a potential breakthrough for improving the efficiency and capability of LLMs in extended interactions. Deep-tech thinkers should explore bio-inspired computing as a fertile ground for overcoming current AI limitations.

### **[MobileGym: A Verifiable and Highly Parallel Simulation Platform for Mobile GUI Agent Research](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26114v1)**
MobileGym introduces a new browser-hosted, lightweight, and fully controllable simulation environment for mobile GUI agent research, emphasizing interaction fidelity without proprietary backends. This platform offers researchers a powerful tool for developing and testing mobile AI agents in a verifiable and highly parallel manner. It promises to accelerate innovation in mobile AI by providing a standardized, accessible, and efficient research environment.

### **[Looped Diffusion Language Models](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26106v1)**
This paper explores Looped Diffusion Language Models as a promising alternative to autoregressive models for language generation, focusing on effective transformer architectures for Masked Diffusion Models (MDMs). Researchers should delve into this as a novel paradigm for language modeling, potentially offering different trade-offs in terms of generation quality, diversity, and computational efficiency compared to current state-of-the-art methods. This could open new avenues for text generation and understanding.

### **[SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23904v2)**
SkillOpt presents a new executive strategy for self-evolving agent skills, treating skill acquisition as a deep-learning optimization problem rather than relying on hand-crafting or loose self-revision. This research offers a significant step towards more autonomous and capable AI agents that can continuously learn and refine their abilities. Academics should consider the implications for creating truly adaptive and general-purpose AI systems.
