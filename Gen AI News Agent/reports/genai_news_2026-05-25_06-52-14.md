## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[LLMs as Noisy Channels: A Shannon Perspective on Model Capacity and Scaling Laws](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23901v1)**
This paper introduces a novel Shannon-theoretic perspective on LLMs, challenging conventional monotonic scaling laws by explaining non-monotonic phenomena like catastrophic overtraining. Researchers should delve into this work to gain a deeper theoretical understanding of LLM capacity limits and to inform more robust training and optimization strategies that account for these complex behaviors.

### **[Is Capability a Liability? More Capable Language Models Make Worse Forecasts When It Matters Most](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22672v2)**
A critical finding reveals that highly capable LLMs exhibit inverse scaling on forecasting problems with superlinear growth and tail risk, common in finance and epidemiology. This research is crucial for academics and practitioners developing AI for high-stakes decision-making, highlighting that raw capability does not always equate to reliability and demanding a re-evaluation of how LLMs are applied in critical predictive tasks.

### **[Constraint Decay: The Fragility of LLM Agents in Back End Code Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06445)**
This study exposes the "constraint decay" phenomenon, where LLM agents struggle to maintain complex constraints during back-end code generation, leading to fragile outputs. Researchers must address this fundamental fragility to build more reliable and robust AI agents, particularly for critical applications like automated software development where adherence to specifications is paramount.

### **[SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23904v1)**
SkillOpt proposes a new framework for the self-evolution of agent skills, moving beyond hand-crafted or one-shot generation methods. This research offers a promising direction for developing truly autonomous and adaptive AI agents, providing a "deep-learning optimizer" for skills that could significantly advance the capabilities and versatility of AI systems.

### **[Complete-muE: Optimal Hyperparameter Transfer and Scaling for MoE Models](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23893v1)**
Complete-muE introduces a framework for optimal hyperparameter transfer across dense FFN and Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models, addressing limitations of existing tools like $\mu$P. This work is vital for researchers working with large-scale MoE architectures, offering a path to more efficient training, better generalization, and simplified hyperparameter tuning for these complex models.
