## 🔬 Research Frontiers

### **[In Harvard study, AI offered more accurate emergency room diagnoses than two human doctors](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/03/in-harvard-study-ai-offered-more-accurate-diagnoses-than-emergency-room-doctors/)**
A groundbreaking Harvard study reveals that large language models can outperform human doctors in emergency room diagnoses, signaling a profound shift in medical AI capabilities. This research suggests AI's potential to significantly enhance diagnostic accuracy in critical care, pushing the boundaries of clinical application and raising new questions about human-AI collaboration in healthcare.

### **[HyCOP: Hybrid Composition Operators for Interpretable Learning of PDEs](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00820v1)**
Researchers introduce HyCOP, a novel modular framework designed for interpretable learning of parametric PDE solution operators through the composition of simple modules. This work advances scientific AI by offering a more transparent and structured approach to modeling complex physical systems, promising greater insights and reliability in fields like fluid dynamics and materials science.

### **[When LLMs Stop Following Steps: A Diagnostic Study of Procedural Execution in Language Models](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00817v1)**
This diagnostic study investigates a critical limitation of LLMs: their occasional failure to faithfully execute multi-step procedures specified in prompts, despite achieving high final-answer accuracy. This research is crucial for understanding the robustness and reliability of LLMs in complex reasoning tasks, paving the way for more dependable AI agents that can consistently follow instructions.

### **[Persistent Visual Memory: Sustaining Perception for Deep Generation in LVLMs](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00814v1)**
This paper addresses the "Visual Signal Dilution" phenomenon in Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs), where visual information degrades over long textual contexts. The proposed concept of "Persistent Visual Memory" aims to sustain perception for deep generation, offering a novel approach to enhance multimodal understanding and improve the long-context reasoning capabilities of LVLMs.

### **[Can Coding Agents Reproduce Findings in Computational Materials Science?](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00803v1)**
Researchers explore the capability of autonomous coding agents to reproduce findings in computational materials science, questioning whether their strong performance on software engineering benchmarks translates to scientific discovery. This study probes the potential for AI to accelerate scientific research by automating complex computational tasks, while also highlighting the need to validate their reliability in rigorous scientific contexts.
